# Collection Selection Philosophy

## **What Qualifies a Text for Project Parallax**

**Universal Qualifications**

Every text in the Project Parallax Library meets these criteria:

**1. Public Domain Status**

- All texts are in the public domain (published before 1928 or explicitly released)
- Freely available through Project Gutenberg, Sacred Texts Archive, or Internet Archive
- No copyright restrictions; accessible to all users

**2. Parallax Value**

- Each text bridges multiple perspectives (tagged with 2–4 lenses)
- Reveals connections across traditions, not isolated knowledge
- Examples:
- *The Varieties of Religious Experience* (James) bridges psychological, religious/spiritual, and philosophical lenses
- *The Kybalion* bridges symbolic/occult, philosophical, and historical/anthropological perspectives
- *Harmonices Mundi* (Kepler) connects mathematical, scientific, philosophical, and symbolic/occult lenses

**3. Foundational Influence**

- Texts that shaped traditions, shifted paradigms, or opened new ways of seeing
- Priority 1 texts are foundational (e.g., *The Republic*, *The Bhagavad Gita*, *On the Origin of Species*)
- Priority 2–4 texts build on or complement these foundations

**4. Accessibility**

- Readable and approachable, not just historically important
- Many chosen for being "accessible," "readable," or "perfect beginner text"
- Balance of short, accessible works and deeper texts

**5. Cross-Tradition Synthesis**

- Texts that show how different wisdom paths express similar truths
- Examples:
- *The Golden Bough* reveals ritual patterns across cultures
- *Isis Unveiled* synthesizes Eastern and Western esotericism
- *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* is an encyclopedia showing breadth of occult knowledge

**The Seven-Lens Balance**

Each text is tagged with one or more of these lenses:

- **Scientific** — Physics, biology, cosmology, empirical evidence
- **Psychological** — Jungian, cognitive science, archetypes, depth psychology
- **Philosophical** — Metaphysics, ethics, epistemology, ontology
- **Religious/Spiritual** — Comparative theology, mysticism, sacred texts
- **Historical/Anthropological** — Cultural evolution, mythology, ritual context
- **Symbolic/Occult** — Correspondences, alchemy, astrology, esoteric systems
- **Mathematical** — Sacred geometry, numerology, patterns, universal ratios

Most texts span multiple lenses, showing convergence.

**Selection Criteria in Practice**

**Priority 1 Texts (Foundational):**

- Essential for understanding a tradition or perspective
- Highly accessible and influential
- Bridge multiple lenses
- Examples: *The Kybalion*, *Meditations*, *The Tao Te Ching*, *The Republic*

**Priority 2–4 Texts (Supporting):**

- Build on foundational texts
- Fill gaps in lens representation
- Provide depth in specific domains
- Examples: *The Zohar*, *The Tibetan Book of the Dead*, *The Divine Proportion*

**What Each Text Brings**

**Parallax Value:**

- Shows how traditions connect (e.g., *The Varieties of Religious Experience* bridges psychology and spirituality)
- Reveals universal patterns (e.g., *The Golden Bough* shows ritual patterns across cultures)
- Demonstrates cross-tradition synthesis (e.g., *Isis Unveiled* synthesizes Eastern and Western esotericism)

**Influence and Significance:**

- Shaped human thought, practice, or understanding
- Influenced entire traditions or shifted paradigms
- Examples: *On the Origin of Species*, *The Interpretation of Dreams*, *The Bhagavad Gita*

**Lens Balance:**

- Contributes to the seven-lens framework
- Ensures no single perspective dominates
- Mathematical lens intentionally balanced (12% of texts) to include sacred geometry and numerology

**Accessibility:**

- Readable and approachable
- Many chosen for being "accessible," "short," or "perfect beginner text"
- Examples: *Meditations* ("Most accessible ancient philosophy"), *The Kybalion* ("Short and accessible")

**The Philosophy: Not an Archive, But a Curated Synthesis**

This collection is not comprehensive. It is curated to reveal convergence.

**What We Prioritize:**

- Texts that bridge multiple perspectives
- Works that have influenced other traditions
- Books that demonstrate cross-tradition patterns
- Content that illuminates the seven-lens framework

**What We Don't Prioritize:**

- Completeness for its own sake
- Texts that exist in isolation
- Works that don't serve the convergence mission

**The Result:**

- Smaller, curated collection (100+ texts vs. millions in archives)
- Rich metadata (lens tags, domain classifications, cross-references)
- Interconnected structure (wikilinks, correspondence tables, Parallax Graph)
- AI-powered synthesis (seven-lens reasoning, semantic search)

**Public Domain Commitment**

Every text is:

- In the public domain (published before 1928 or explicitly released)
- Available through free sources:
- **Project Gutenberg** — Most texts
- **Sacred Texts Archive** — Esoteric, religious, and mythological texts
- **Internet Archive** — Historical and reference works
- Free to use, share, and digitize
- Accessible to all users without paywalls

This commitment ensures:

- Universal access regardless of economic status
- Legal clarity for digitization and AI processing
- Alignment with the mission of making hidden wisdom accessible

**The Selection Process**

Each text was evaluated for:

1. **Parallax Value** — Does it reveal patterns when viewed alongside other traditions?
2. **Influence** — Has it shaped human thought or practice?
3. **Lens Contribution** — Does it contribute to the seven-lens framework?
4. **Accessibility** — Is it readable and approachable?
5. **Public Domain Status** — Is it freely available?

The "Why_Chosen" column explains the reasoning for each text, showing how it serves the convergence mission.

**The Result: A Living Collection**

This is not a static archive. It's a curated synthesis that:

- Grows organically based on Parallax Value
- Balances all seven lenses
- Honors all wisdom traditions equally
- Reveals how all paths converge toward understanding ourselves, our world, and our universe

Every text was chosen not because it's the "best" in its category, but because it serves the mission of revealing convergence across wisdom traditions—showing how all paths meet in the search for understanding.
